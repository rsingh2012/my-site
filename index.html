Here's what clients actually get when they work with me: a team that builds technically rigorous groundwater models — and a leader who will tell them honestly what those models can and can't prove. That's a shorter list than most consultants advertise, but it's a harder standard to meet.

I'm Abhishek Singh. I have a PhD in Environmental Engineering from the University of Illinois, a PE license, and two decades of experience on some of the most complex water problems in the US. I started at INTERA as a groundwater modeler in Austin — eight years working on major regional aquifer systems in Texas and on federal projects with long-term, high-consequence stakes. That's where the technical foundation was built.

In 2015, I relocated to California and built INTERA's practice here from the ground up — ten years in one of the most demanding regulatory and hydrogeologic environments in the country. I now lead the company's Water Resources & Supply line of business and serve on INTERA's Board of Directors.

My clients include major California water agencies — Orange County Water District, Water Replenishment District of Southern CA, Calleguas Municipal Water District, West Basin MWD — as well as state and federal agencies including CA DWR, SWRCB, USBR, and USACE.

I also serve as Vice President of the Groundwater Resources Association of California and chair its Technical Committee. I'm not watching California water policy from the outside.

The thing I'm most direct about with clients: uncertainty is real, and pretending otherwise doesn't serve anyone. A well-calibrated model that hides its own limitations is not a defensible model. My teams are trained to communicate what the science actually shows — and what it doesn't. That's what holds up in court. That's what regulators trust. And that's what good decisions get made on.

01

Groundwater Science & Modeling

  • Models that reflect the actual hydrogeology — not just models that calibrate well on paper
  • Stochastic uncertainty analysis and multi-model averaging, so decision-makers understand the confidence bounds around any answer
  • Seawater intrusion modeling and barrier project support — critical for coastal agencies managing intrusion risk
  • Subsidence monitoring, modeling, and management — including litigation-ready documentation
  • PFAS monitoring and contaminant migration modeling
  • Unsaturated and vadose zone analysis
The outcome

Technical work product that holds up — to regulators, to peer review, and in contested proceedings.

02

Water Resources Strategy & Planning

  • SGMA compliance and GSP development — basin assessment, data gap analysis, stakeholder coordination, and regulatory interface, built to survive both DWR review and legal challenge
  • Managed aquifer recharge (MAR) and aquifer storage and recovery (ASR) planning — feasibility through implementation
  • Brackish groundwater supply development — an underutilized asset for agencies facing supply constraints
  • Climate change impact modeling for long-range supply planning — quantified risk, not qualitative hedging
  • Decision-support tools and web-based model viewers that make technical outputs usable by non-technical decision-makers
The outcome

Strategies grounded in science that agency boards can adopt, regulators can approve, and the public can understand.

03

Expert Support & Regulatory Interface

  • Technical expert witness services in groundwater quantity and quality litigation — I've testified and provided expert declarations in contested water proceedings
  • Regulatory coordination across CA DWR, SWRCB, DTSC, EPA, USBR, and USACE
  • Independent peer review of third-party modeling work — for agencies, counsel, or boards evaluating technical claims from other parties
  • Technical advisory support for organizations navigating complex groundwater questions without a full in-house technical team
The outcome

A credentialed, credible technical voice that performs under scrutiny — not just in the report, but in the room.
